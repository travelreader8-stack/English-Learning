# Lesson 13 Plan - The Greenwood Boys

This is the source-of-truth design card for producing Lesson 13. A development
thread should read `AGENTS.md`, then `LESSON_PRODUCTION_PLAN.md`, then this file
before producing any Lesson 13 assets.

## Basic Info

- Lesson: 13
- Title: `The Greenwood Boys`
- Chinese title: `绿林少年`
- Core bridge: `这不是单纯介绍一个乐队，而是一次流行组合巡演到小镇：年轻人去车站迎接，晚上去俱乐部看演出，警察要维持秩序。整篇课文像一张未来几天的活动时间表。`

## Production Warnings

This lesson can feel hard to memorize because it does not have a strong
punchline. Production should make the hidden timetable visible.

- Do not frame the lesson as a generic music lesson.
  - The main teaching object is the future event schedule.
  - The band is the reason the schedule exists, not the whole point.
- Do not over-explain whether `The Greenwood Boys` is a real group.
  - Treat it as a pop-singer group / boy band in the story.
  - The slightly old-fashioned name can be used lightly, but do not mock it.
- `Workers' Club` needs a culture bridge.
  - Explain it as an old-style local workers' club / community club where
    people could gather and performances could happen.
  - Do not let the student think it is a school club or an online fan club.
- `The police will have a difficult time` is about crowd control, not crime.
  - Explain that many excited young fans may rush, crowd, block roads, or make
    the station and club hard to manage.
  - Keep the tone lively and orderly, not dangerous or violent.
- The lesson should not moralize about fandom.
  - Connect to concerts, idol groups, fans waiting at stations or airports, and
    event security.
  - The point is excitement plus order, not "追星不好".
- Student-facing display:
  - Prefer `绿林少年` as the Chinese title.
  - If explaining `Workers' Club`, use `工人俱乐部 / 社区活动场所` early enough.

If production requires editing `web/data/lessons.json`, stop and ask for a
shared-data edit because it is outside the normal per-lesson write set.

## Lesson Positioning

This lesson is about a town preparing for a pop group visit.

The Greenwood Boys are a group of pop singers. They are travelling around the
country now. Tomorrow they will arrive in this town by train. Most young people
in the town will meet them at the station. Tomorrow evening, the group will sing
at the Workers' Club. They will stay for five days and give five performances.
Because crowds of young fans will come, the police will have to keep order.

The courseware should make the student understand:

`future schedule = tomorrow arrival -> station welcome -> evening performance -> five-day stay -> five shows -> crowd control`

The ending should land as: "Every time a popular group comes, the same thing
happens: young people get excited, and the police have to keep order."

## One-Sentence Hook

A famous pop group is coming to town tomorrow, and the whole town already knows
the timetable: train, station, club, five shows, and a very busy police force.

## Story Focus

The timetable chain is:

1. The Greenwood Boys are a group of pop singers.
2. At present, they are visiting all parts of the country.
3. Tomorrow they will arrive here.
4. They will come by train.
5. Most young people in town will meet them at the station.
6. Tomorrow evening they will sing at the Workers' Club.
7. They will stay for five days.
8. During the stay, they will give five performances.
9. As usual, the police will have a difficult time.
10. The police will try to keep order.
11. It is always the same on these occasions.

The production should make this feel like a visible event board, not a list of
random future sentences.

## Culture Bridge

This section should appear early in the script.

Explain:

- `The Greenwood Boys` are a pop-singer group in the story, similar to a boy
  band or idol group.
- `all parts of the country` means they are touring around many places, not
  staying in one city.
- `by train` fits an older British small-town touring scene. Today the modern
  version might be a group arriving by high-speed rail or plane.
- `station` is important because fans go there to see the group arrive.
- `Workers' Club` is a local club / community venue, not a school club.
- `police keep order` means they keep the crowd safe and organized.

Suggested student-friendly bridge:

`你可以把这篇想成一个小镇版的演唱会行程表：一个流行组合明天坐火车来，年轻粉丝去车站接，晚上去一个本地俱乐部演出，接下来五天还有五场表演。人一多，警察就要维持秩序。`

## Modern Bridge

Connect the old scene to present-day experiences:

- a popular group announces a city stop in a tour schedule
- fans watching arrival photos or videos online
- people going to a station, airport, or venue to see someone famous
- concert schedules, ticket times, venue opening times, and crowd-control rules
- security staff and police guiding crowds at a big event
- school events where many students gather and teachers need to keep order

Do not make the modern bridge only about celebrity culture. The more durable
pattern is:

`popular event -> excited crowd -> clear schedule -> people responsible for order`

## Main Language Focus

The key grammar is `will be doing`.

Teach it as a future timetable, not as a dry tense label.

- `They will be arriving here tomorrow.`
- `They will be coming by train.`
- `Most of the young people ... will be meeting them at the station.`
- `Tomorrow evening they will be singing at the Workers' Club.`
- `The Greenwood Boys will be staying for five days.`
- `They will be trying to keep order.`

Student-facing explanation:

`will be doing` often feels like a camera placed in the future. You are not only
saying "this will happen"; you are imagining that at that future time, the
action will be in progress.

Use the timetable metaphor:

- Tomorrow: arriving
- On the way: coming by train
- At the station: fans meeting them
- Tomorrow evening: singing
- For five days: staying
- During the crowds: police trying to keep order

Contrast only lightly with `will do`:

- `They will give five performances.` = count the completed performances.
- `They will be singing at the Workers' Club.` = show the action happening at
  that future time.

Do not overload the lesson with advanced tense names.

## Vocabulary Plan

Formal vocab blocks should be exactly 3:

1. `group`
   - Meaning: a set of people together; here, a singer group.
   - Bridge: not any random crowd, but a performing group with members.
   - Example: `The Greenwood Boys are a group of pop singers.`
2. `performance`
   - Meaning: a show given for an audience.
   - Bridge: a concert, stage show, school show, or public performance.
   - Example: `They will give five performances.`
3. `occasion`
   - Meaning: a special time or situation when something happens.
   - Bridge: a concert arrival, school ceremony, big match, or public event.
   - Example: `It is always the same on these occasions.`

Supporting vocabulary to introduce in context, not as full vocab cards:

- `pop singers` = 流行歌手
- `at present` = 目前，现在
- `all parts of the country` = 全国各地
- `station` = 车站
- `Workers' Club` = 工人俱乐部 / 社区活动场所
- `as usual` = 像往常一样
- `keep order` = 维持秩序

## Scene And Script Direction

The script should feel like a lively event announcement.

Recommended sequence:

1. Hook: "A pop group is coming tomorrow, and the town already has a schedule."
2. Explain the old context: touring group, train arrival, local club.
3. Show the modern bridge: concert tour, station/airport fans, event security.
4. Retell in 4 frames:
   - Frame 1: a poster or news board announces The Greenwood Boys tour.
   - Frame 2: fans gather at the train station before the group arrives.
   - Frame 3: the group performs at the Workers' Club in the evening.
   - Frame 4: police and staff guide an excited crowd and keep order.
5. Discussion: why this text feels like a timetable.
6. Passage reading.
7. Vocab: `group`, `performance`, `occasion`.
8. Grammar: `will be doing` as a camera in the future.
9. Living-scene transfer.
10. Outro: practice order, including read-aloud steps.

## Visual Direction

Keep visuals bright and lively, but controlled.

- Use a small-town concert / train-station arrival mood.
- Show excited young fans, but no unsafe pushing or chaos.
- The Workers' Club should look like a modest local venue, not a giant stadium.
- Police/security should look like they are organizing a crowd, not responding
  to a crime scene.
- Avoid modern idol branding that looks like a real band or celebrity.
- Avoid making the Greenwood Boys too specific; they can be a generic pop group.

Four storyboard frames:

1. Tour announcement board or poster in town: "The Greenwood Boys".
2. Train station scene with young fans waiting.
3. Evening performance at a local club venue.
4. Police/security keeping an excited crowd in orderly lines.

## Living-Scene Exercise

The student-facing transfer should be about a future event schedule.

Good prompts:

- `假如你明天要去看一场喜欢的演出/比赛/活动，你能用英语说出时间表吗？`
- `明天谁会到？你会在哪里等？晚上会发生什么？`
- `如果学校来了一个很受欢迎的人，大家会在哪里集合？谁来维持秩序？`

Expected scaffold:

`Tomorrow, ___ will be arriving at ___. I will be meeting ___ at ___. In the evening, ___ will be ___.`

Keep this exercise concrete. The student should produce a small schedule, not a
general opinion about music.

## Read-Aloud Guidance

Choose 5-8 sentences that carry the timetable and `will be doing` rhythm.

Recommended sentences:

1. `The Greenwood Boys are a group of pop singers.`
   - Focus: `group of pop singers`
2. `At present, they are visiting all parts of the country.`
   - Focus: `At present`, `all parts of the country`
3. `They will be arriving here tomorrow.`
   - Focus: `will be arriving`
4. `They will be coming by train and most of the young people in the town will be meeting them at the station.`
   - Focus: the long sentence, two `will be doing` actions
5. `Tomorrow evening they will be singing at the Workers' Club.`
   - Focus: `Tomorrow evening`, `will be singing`
6. `The Greenwood Boys will be staying for five days.`
   - Focus: `will be staying`
7. `During this time, they will give five performances.`
   - Focus: `During this time`, `five performances`
8. `They will be trying to keep order.`
   - Focus: `trying to keep order`

The read-aloud section should help the student hear the repeating rhythm:

`will be arriving / will be coming / will be meeting / will be singing / will be staying / will be trying`

## Grammar Handling

Do not present grammar before the story. Let the student first understand the
event schedule, then name the pattern.

Suggested explanation:

`这篇课文最难记的地方，是它不像故事反转，而像一张活动时间表。作者不断用 will be doing，把未来某个时间正在发生的动作一个个摆出来。你只要按时间表记，就顺了。`

Use a compact table in the script or display:

| Time / Place | Sentence Pattern | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| tomorrow | will be arriving | 明天会到达 |
| by train | will be coming | 会坐火车来 |
| at the station | will be meeting | 会在车站迎接 |
| tomorrow evening | will be singing | 明晚会演唱 |
| for five days | will be staying | 会待五天 |
| during the crowds | will be trying | 会努力维持秩序 |

## Tone

Light, energetic, and organized.

The narration can use concert/fan energy, but keep it calm enough for a family
learning tool. Avoid noisy slang or aggressive crowd descriptions.

The child should feel:

- "I understand why everyone is excited."
- "I can see the event schedule."
- "The repeated grammar helps me remember the whole passage."

## Acceptance Criteria For Production

Lesson 13 production is successful only if:

- The script clearly frames the text as an event timetable.
- The culture bridge explains `Workers' Club` and crowd control.
- The modern bridge connects to concerts, fan arrivals, event schedules, and
  security/order.
- The 4 retell frames show announcement, station arrival, club performance, and
  crowd order.
- The 3 formal vocab blocks are `group`, `performance`, and `occasion`.
- Supporting words include `pop singers`, `at present`, `all parts of the
  country`, `Workers' Club`, `as usual`, and `keep order`.
- The grammar section teaches `will be doing` through the timetable/camera
  metaphor.
- The living-scene exercise asks the student to describe a future event
  schedule.
- The outro includes the full practice order and the concrete read-aloud steps:
  `⓪ 跟读：听课文全文 -> 听原音 -> 录一句 -> 获取反馈`, then
  `① 生活场景`, `② 完形`, `③ 中译英`, `④ 英译中`, `⑤ 默写`.
