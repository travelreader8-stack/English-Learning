## Basic Info

- Lesson: 32
- Title: `Shopping made easy`
- Chinese title: `购物变得很方便`
- Core bridge: `这不是教“购物小技巧”，而是一个反讽故事：母亲假装购物，女儿作为店员帮她把昂贵裙子包好，最后侦探发现所谓的 free dress 其实是串通偷窃。`

## Production Warnings

This lesson should be about rule-breaking being discovered, not about making
the theft feel clever or attractive.

- Explain the title as irony.
  - Normal meaning: shopping becomes easier.
  - Story meaning: the woman makes shopping "easy" by not paying.
  - The lesson should make clear that this is illegal and dishonest.
- Explain the quotation marks around `'gave'`.
  - The daughter did not truly give her mother a gift.
  - The quotation marks signal irony: the "free dress" was stolen from the
    store.
- Do not present shoplifting as a trick.
  - The story's surprise is the detective discovering the relationship between
    the woman and the assistant.
  - The moral frame is trust, rules, and honesty in a shop.
- Explain `detective` in the store context.
  - This is not mainly a Sherlock-Holmes detective.
  - It is closer to a store detective, security worker, or loss-prevention
    person.
- Explain `assistant` in the shop context.
  - Here it means shop assistant / sales assistant / store clerk.
  - It is not a general office assistant.
- Explain `article`.
  - Here `articles` means items or goods in a shop.
  - It does not mean written articles.
- Explain the old-fashioned opening carefully.
  - `People are not so honest as they once were` sounds like a general complaint.
  - Do not turn it into "people today are all dishonest."
  - Treat it as the narrator's setup for a shoplifting story.
- Keep the visuals calm.
  - No police drama, handcuffs close-ups, or frightening arrest scene.
  - Focus on observation, store layout, parcel, payment counter, and reveal.
- Do not edit `web/data/lessons.json` during normal lesson production unless
  the user explicitly asks for a shared-data fix.

## Lesson Positioning

This lesson is about a store detective noticing a repeated pattern and
discovering a family-based shoplifting trick.

The narrator begins by saying that people are not as honest as they once were
and that the temptation to steal is greater than ever, especially in large
shops. A detective has recently watched a well-dressed woman who always goes
into a large store on Monday mornings. One Monday, there are fewer people in the
shop than usual, so it is easier for the detective to watch her. The woman first
buys a few small articles. Then she chooses one of the most expensive dresses in
the shop and hands it to an assistant, who wraps it up for her quickly. The
woman takes the parcel and walks out without paying. When she is arrested, the
detective finds out that the shop assistant is her daughter. The daughter has
been "giving" her mother a free dress once a week.

The courseware should make the student understand:

`store detective -> repeated Monday visits -> fewer people -> small purchases -> expensive dress -> assistant wraps parcel -> woman leaves without paying -> arrest -> assistant is daughter -> "free dress" was theft`

The ending should land as: "It was not easy shopping. It was an inside helper
making stealing look like shopping."

## One-Sentence Hook

The woman seemed to be shopping, but the detective discovered that the shop
assistant was secretly helping her steal dresses.

## Story Focus

The detective-observation chain is:

1. The narrator says people are not so honest as they once were.
2. The temptation to steal is greater than ever before.
3. A detective watches a well-dressed woman.
4. The woman always enters a large store on Monday mornings.
5. One Monday, there are fewer people in the shop than usual.
6. This makes it easier for the detective to watch her.
7. The woman first buys a few small articles.
8. After a little time, she chooses one of the most expensive dresses.
9. She hands the dress to an assistant.
10. The assistant wraps it up as quickly as possible.
11. The woman simply takes the parcel and leaves without paying.
12. She is arrested.
13. The detective finds out that the shop assistant is her daughter.
14. The daughter has been "giving" her mother a free dress once a week.

The production should make the contrast visible:

`normal shopping behavior` versus `hidden theft arrangement`.

## Culture And Store Bridge

This section should appear early in the script.

Explain:

- A large store can be busy, with many customers and assistants.
- A shop assistant helps customers, wraps items, and usually sends them to pay
  or handles payment.
- A store detective or security worker may watch for theft in a large shop.
- In modern shops, cameras, alarms, receipts, checkout counters, and security
  staff all help protect store property.
- Walking out with goods without paying is stealing, even if a family member in
  the store helps.
- A "free dress" from the store is not a gift unless the store has actually
  agreed to give it.

Suggested student-friendly bridge:

`这课里的 detective 不是拿放大镜破案的侦探，而更像今天商场里的防损员或安保人员。他发现一个穿得很讲究的女人每周一都来店里。她先买小东西，然后挑一件很贵的裙子，店员飞快包好，她不付钱就走。最后反转是：这个 shop assistant 其实是她女儿，所以 free dress 不是礼物，而是串通偷东西。`

## Modern Bridge

Connect the large-store scene to present-day shopping:

- shopping malls and large department stores
- store cameras and security staff
- checkout counters and receipts
- self-checkout and the trust behind scanning items
- shop assistants wrapping items or putting them in shopping bags
- family members or friends not being allowed to "give away" store property
- online shopping with payment confirmation before delivery
- the difference between a discount, a gift, and stealing

The durable pattern is:

`trust in shopping -> hidden rule-breaking -> observation -> reveal -> consequence`

Do not make this a detailed crime-prevention lesson. Keep it focused on honesty,
payment, and why rules make shared shopping possible.

## Main Language Focus

The main grammar is comparison, especially comparative structures that build
the detective situation.

Teach these through the story:

- `not so honest as they once were`
  - Compare people now with people in the past, from the narrator's viewpoint.
- `greater than ever before`
  - The temptation is described as bigger than before.
- `fewer people in the shop than usual`
  - There are not as many people as on a normal day.
- `easier for the detective to watch her`
  - Fewer people makes the detective's job easier.
- `one of the most expensive dresses`
  - The dress is among the highest-priced dresses in the shop.
- `as quickly as possible`
  - The assistant wraps it as fast as she can.

Student-facing explanation:

This lesson uses comparison to build the scene. There are fewer people, so it is
easier to watch. The woman chooses one of the most expensive dresses, and the
assistant wraps it as quickly as possible. These comparative phrases make the
detective's discovery feel logical.

Use a compact board:

| Phrase | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| not so honest as... | less honest than before |
| greater than ever before | bigger than at any earlier time |
| fewer... than usual | not as many as normal |
| easier for... to... | less difficult for someone |
| one of the most expensive... | among the highest-priced items |
| as quickly as possible | as fast as she can |

Secondary language points:

- `without paying`
  - She leaves and does not pay.
- `When she was arrested`
  - Passive voice focuses on what happened to her.
- `found out that...`
  - discovered the truth.
- `once a week`
  - one time every week.
- quotation marks around `'gave'`
  - ironic, not a true gift.

## Vocabulary Plan

Formal vocab blocks should be exactly 3:

1. `temptation`
   - Meaning: a strong feeling that you want to do something, especially
     something wrong or unwise.
   - Bridge: temptation to steal, temptation to cheat, temptation to eat candy
     before dinner.
   - Example: `The temptation to steal is greater than ever before.`
2. `article`
   - Meaning in this lesson: an item or object for sale in a shop.
   - Bridge: small articles = small goods/items; not a written article.
   - Example: `The woman first bought a few small articles.`
3. `arrest`
   - Meaning: when police or security take someone because they may have broken
     the law.
   - Bridge: she was arrested after leaving without paying.
   - Example: `When she was arrested, the detective found out the truth.`

Supporting vocabulary to introduce in context, not as full vocab cards:

- `once` = at one time in the past
- `honest` = truthful and fair; not stealing or cheating
- `steal` = take something that is not yours
- `detective` = store detective / security observer in this lesson
- `well-dressed` = wearing nice clothes
- `large store` = big shop / department store
- `fewer` = not as many
- `than usual` = compared with the normal situation
- `assistant` = shop assistant / sales assistant
- `wrap up` = cover or package something
- `parcel` = wrapped package
- `simply` = just / without doing anything else
- `without paying` = not paying money for the goods
- `shop assistant` = store clerk / sales assistant
- `free` = costing no money, but here ironic
- `once a week` = one time every week

## Scene And Script Direction

The script should feel like a calm store-detective reveal.

Recommended sequence:

1. Hook: "The shopping was easy because someone inside the store was helping."
2. Explain the title irony and make the ethical frame clear.
3. Bridge large store, shop assistant, store detective, and modern security.
4. Retell in 4 frames:
   - Frame 1: a large store on Monday morning; detective watches a well-dressed

   - Frame 2: the woman buys small articles, then chooses an expensive dress.
   - Frame 3: the assistant wraps the dress quickly; the woman leaves without

   - Frame 4: detective reveal: the assistant is the woman's daughter; the

5. Discussion: why the quotation marks around `'gave'` matter.
6. Passage reading.
7. Vocab: `temptation`, `article`, `arrest`.
8. Grammar: comparison ladder and passive `was arrested`.
9. Living-scene transfer.
10. Outro: practice order, including read-aloud steps.

## Visual Direction

Keep the visuals clear, modern enough to bridge, and ethically calm.

- Main woman:
  - Well-dressed adult woman, composed, carrying small purchases.
  - Do not glamorize her or make the theft look stylish.
- Detective:
  - Store detective / security observer, calm and observant.
  - Not a dramatic police detective with trench coat unless stylized lightly.
- Assistant:
  - Young shop assistant behind a counter or wrapping desk.
  - Later reveal that she is the woman's daughter.
- Setting:
  - Large department store with clothing area, dress rack, wrapping counter,
    checkout / exit area.
  - Use visual cues for store layout rather than readable signs.
- Frame mood:
  - Frame 1: observation.
  - Frame 2: normal-looking shopping.
  - Frame 3: suspicious exit without paying.
  - Frame 4: reveal and consequence.
- Avoid showing theft as exciting.
- Avoid police violence, handcuffs close-ups, or frightening arrest.
- No readable text is required.

## Four-Frame Storyboard Intent

Frame 1: Monday Morning Observation

- A large store with fewer shoppers than usual.
- The detective watches a well-dressed woman enter.
- The mood is calm, not dramatic.
- The visual question: "Why is the detective watching her?"

Frame 2: Small Articles And Expensive Dress

- The woman buys a few small items.
- Then she chooses an expensive dress.
- Show the contrast between small purchases and the expensive dress.
- The visual idea: "This looks like normal shopping, but something is off."

Frame 3: Wrapped Parcel Without Payment

- The assistant wraps the dress quickly.
- The woman takes the parcel and walks toward the exit.
- Make it clear she bypasses payment / checkout, without needing readable text.
- The detective notices.

Frame 4: The Reveal

- The detective has discovered the truth.
- The assistant is shown as the woman's daughter.
- The "free dress" idea should feel ironic, not celebratory.
- The image should imply consequence and dishonesty being found out.

## Living-Scene Exercise Direction

The living-scene exercise should transfer the lesson into honest shopping and
checkout behavior.

Recommended scenario:

`You are shopping in a large store. You pick up an item, but you must pay before leaving. A friend says, "Just take it." You explain why that is wrong and what to do instead.`

Student should practice:

- distinguishing buying, receiving a real gift, and stealing
- saying that something must be paid for
- refusing a dishonest suggestion
- explaining that a shop assistant cannot give away store property
- using comparison phrases in a simple way

Useful expressions:

- `We must pay for it first.`
- `We can't walk out without paying.`
- `That is not a free gift.`
- `The shop assistant cannot give it away.`
- `It is better to be honest.`
- `Let's go to the checkout.`

The exercise should be firm but not frightening. Keep it about everyday honesty.

## Suggested You Too Scene

Scene:

`You and a friend are in a large store. Your friend thinks it is easy to take a small item without paying. You explain the rule and guide them to the checkout.`

Prompt ideas:

- `如果朋友说“不付钱拿走也没人发现”，你怎么回答？`
- `什么是真正的 free gift？什么是偷拿？`
- `为什么店员不能随便把商店的东西送给亲人？`

Target output pattern:

`We can't take it without paying. It is not a free gift. The shop owns it, so we must go to the checkout first. It is better to be honest.`

Keep the focus on rule, trust, and repair.

## Grammar Board

Keep the grammar board tied to comparison and the reveal.

Board structure:

`Comparison ladder`

- `not so honest as they once were`
- `greater than ever before`
- `fewer people than usual`
- `easier for the detective to watch her`
- `one of the most expensive dresses`
- `as quickly as possible`

`Passive voice`

- `When she was arrested`
- Focus: what happened to the woman, not who arrested her.

`Irony marks`

- `'gave'`
- The quotation marks show it was not a real gift.

Use the final contrast:

`A real gift is given by the owner.`

`A shop assistant cannot give away the store's dress.`

Avoid a long lecture about crime. The grammar should make the detective chain
and final irony clearer.
