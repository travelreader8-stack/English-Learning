## Basic Info

- Lesson: 45
- Title: `A clear conscience`
- Chinese title: `问心无愧`
- Core bridge: `这不是侦探抓小偷的故事，而是一堂“良心分期付款”的幽默课：一个人拿了 Sam 的钱，却一点点把钱还回来，并用 50%、25%、100% 这种荒唐百分比给自己的诚实程度打分。`

## Production Warnings

This lesson should feel morally thoughtful and funny, not like a theft trick.

- Explain `clear conscience` clearly.
  - It means feeling that you have done nothing wrong, or that you have made
    things right.
  - Student-friendly meaning: `心里没有亏欠，晚上能睡得踏实。`
- Do not normalize theft.
  - The notes are funny, but stealing money is still wrong.
  - The person does not become truly honest just by calculating percentages.
  - The lesson should make the joke clear: honesty cannot really be divided into
    `50 per cent`, `25 per cent`, and `100 per cent`.
- Explain the old village / post office background.
  - This is a small-community story: the whole village soon knows the news.
  - A local butcher is a familiar person in the village.
  - In older settings, people might take savings as cash to the post office.
  - Today, many people use bank cards, banking apps, Alipay / WeChat Pay, or
    online transfers instead.
- Keep the unknown thief anonymous.
  - Do not show a clear villain face.
  - Use wrapped parcels, notes, shadows, or a door-step delivery visual to keep
    the mystery.
- Make the passive-voice chain visible.
  - Many important actions hide the doer:
    `had been lost`, `must have been found`, `was not returned`, `had been
    wrapped up`, `was sent`, `was paid back`.
  - This fits the story because the person returning the money stays anonymous.
- Use floating text boxes for chain points.
  - Do not only put key phrases in bottom narration.
  - Important passive phrases, percentages, and note messages can appear as
    short overlay boxes in the illustration.
- Do not edit `web/data/lessons.json` during normal lesson production.

If production requires editing `web/data/lessons.json`, stop and ask for a
shared-data edit because it is outside the normal per-lesson write set.

## Lesson Positioning

This lesson is about a wallet, a guilty conscience, and money paid back in
installments.

Sam Benton, the local butcher, loses his wallet while taking his savings to the
post office. The whole village learns that a large sum of money has been lost.
Sam is sure the wallet must have been found by a villager, but it is not
returned. Three months later, he finds the wallet outside his front door. It is
wrapped in newspaper and contains half the lost money, with a note: `A thief,
yes, but only 50 per cent a thief!` Two months later, more money arrives with a
new note: `Only 25 per cent a thief now!` In time, all the money is paid back in
this way. The last note says: `I am 100 per cent honest now!`

The courseware should make the student understand:

`whole village learns -> butcher loses wallet -> savings to post office -> wallet found but not returned -> three months later -> wrapped wallet at front door -> half money + 50% thief note -> two months later -> more money + 25% thief note -> all money paid back -> 100% honest note`

The ending should land as:

`The notes are funny because the thief treats honesty like a percentage score,
but real honesty is not a math problem.`

## One-Sentence Hook

Someone stole Sam's money, then slowly paid it back while claiming to become
less and less of a thief.

## Village / Butcher / Post Office Background Bridge

This bridge should appear early in the script.

Explain:

- This is a small village story, not a big anonymous city story.
- `The whole village soon learnt` means news travels quickly because people know
  one another.
- `local butcher` means the person who runs the meat shop in the community.
- In older community life, the butcher would be a familiar local figure.
- `savings` means money someone has saved.
- In older settings, people might take cash savings to the post office for
  postal savings, payment, or money services.
- Today, a child may think of bank apps, cards, Alipay, WeChat Pay, or online
  transfers instead.

Suggested student-friendly bridge:

`这课不是现代手机支付时代的故事，而是一个老式小村庄故事。Sam 是本地 butcher，大家认识他；他拿着 savings 去 post office，所以钱包丢了以后，很快 whole village 都知道了。今天我们可能用银行 App 或手机支付，但课文里的时代更依赖现金、钱包和邮局服务。`

Keep the background short but concrete. The goal is to make the lost-wallet
story believable.

## Clear Conscience Bridge

This should be the emotional center of the lesson.

Explain:

- `conscience` is the inner feeling that tells you whether you have done right
  or wrong.
- `a clear conscience` means you do not feel guilty.
- In this story, the thief's conscience is not clear at first.
- The thief tries to make it clear by sending the money back bit by bit.
- The notes are funny because the thief acts as if morality can be measured
  with percentages.

Student-friendly explanation:

`conscience 可以理解成心里的“良心声音”。A clear conscience 就是问心无愧。这个人偷了钱以后，良心不舒服，于是一点一点把钱还回来，还给自己算百分比：50% thief、25% thief、100% honest。好笑的地方是，道德不是数学题，不能真的这样算。`

Suggested visual:

`honesty meter / conscience meter`

- `50% thief`
- `25% thief`
- `100% honest`

Make the meter playful, not moralistic.

## Passive Chain And Floating Text Box Layout

This is a required presentation idea for Lesson 45.

The lesson should not rely only on bottom subtitles. Important grammar and story
nodes should appear inside the illustration as short floating boxes.

Use floating text boxes for:

- `had been lost`
- `must have been found`
- `was not returned`
- `had been wrapped up`
- `50 per cent a thief`
- `25 per cent a thief`
- `was paid back`
- `100 per cent honest`

Purpose:

- The passive phrases show that the doer is hidden.
- The percentage boxes show the thief's absurd self-calculation.
- The student can see the story chain while looking at the picture.

Design guidance:

- Keep each floating box short: a phrase, not a full paragraph.
- Put the box near the matching object or action:
  - wallet: `had been lost`
  - village / unknown finder: `must have been found`
  - front door parcel: `had been wrapped up`
  - note: `50 per cent a thief`
  - later envelope / money: `25 per cent a thief`
  - final note: `100 per cent honest`
- Use arrows or gentle motion if the player supports it.
- Do not clutter the image. Two or three floating boxes per frame is enough.

Generalizable principle:

For lessons with strong action chains or grammar chains, key phrases can be
made visual through illustration labels and floating boxes, not only through
the narration line at the bottom.

## Modern Bridge

Connect the old village story to present-day experiences:

- losing a wallet, phone, transit card, or student card
- finding something that belongs to someone else
- returning lost property through a teacher, security office, front desk, police
  station, or lost-and-found office
- feeling guilty after doing something wrong
- making things right quickly instead of slowly
- understanding that returning half is not the same as being honest

The durable pattern is:

`lost property -> hidden finder -> guilty conscience -> partial repayment ->
more repayment -> full repayment -> comic self-forgiveness`.

Do not over-moralize. Let the humor of the notes carry the lesson, then make
the ethical point cleanly:

`If you find something, return it. Do not keep it and then calculate how honest
you are.`

## Main Language Focus

The main grammar is passive voice and past deduction.

### Passive Voice Chain

Examples:

- `a large sum of money had been lost`
- `the wallet must have been found`
- `it was not returned`
- `It had been wrapped up in newspaper`
- `some more money was sent`
- `all Sam's money was paid back`

Student-facing explanation:

In this story, many sentences use passive voice because the person who found /
took / returned the money is hidden. The grammar matches the mystery: we see
what happened to the wallet and the money, but we do not see who did it.

Use a compact board:

| Passive phrase | What happened | Why passive fits |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `had been lost` | the money was lost | focus on the lost money |
| `must have been found` | someone probably found it | Sam does not know who |
| `was not returned` | nobody brought it back | the finder stays hidden |
| `had been wrapped up` | someone wrapped it | anonymous return |
| `was sent` | someone sent more money | anonymous again |
| `was paid back` | all money returned | result matters |

### `must have been found`

Teach this as a useful deduction:

`Sam was sure that the wallet must have been found by one of the villagers.`

Meaning:

`Sam did not see the person, but he was very sure someone in the village had
found the wallet.`

Form:

`must have been + past participle`

Function:

high-confidence guess about a past passive event.

Student-friendly examples:

- `The door is open. It must have been opened by someone.`
- `The cake is gone. It must have been eaten.`
- `The wallet is missing. It must have been found by someone.`

### Percentage Language

Teach:

- `50 per cent`
- `25 per cent`
- `100 per cent`
- `%` = percent sign

Explain:

- British style often writes `per cent` as two words.
- Modern everyday English often uses `percent` or `%`, especially in data,
  charts, apps, and schoolwork.
- In the story, percentages are used for humor, not real ethics.

Secondary language points:

- `a large sum of money`
  - a large amount of money.
- `wrapped up in newspaper`
  - covered / packaged in newspaper.
- `together with a note`
  - with a note.
- `in time`
  - eventually / after some time.
- `in this way`
  - by this method.
- `paid back`
  - returned money that was owed.

## Percentage Humor Mini-Bridge

This should be playful and memorable.

Explain:

- The notes make the story funny.
- The anonymous person writes as if being a thief can be measured like a math
  score.
- First note: `50 per cent a thief`
- Second note: `25 per cent a thief`
- Final note: `100 per cent honest`

Student-facing explanation:

`他好像在给自己的良心做数学题：还一半，就从 100% 小偷变成 50% 小偷；再还一些，就变成 25% 小偷；最后还清，就宣布自己 100% honest。这个逻辑很荒唐，所以好笑。真正的诚实不是最后算出来的百分比。`

Suggested visual:

`Conscience Meter`

- red zone: `100% thief`
- orange: `50% thief`
- yellow: `25% thief`
- green: `100% honest`

Keep the visual simple and comic, not preachy.

## Grammar Board

Keep the grammar board tied to hidden action and conscience.

Board structure:

`Passive mystery`

- `a large sum of money had been lost`
- `the wallet must have been found`
- `it was not returned`
- `it had been wrapped up`
- `some more money was sent`
- `all Sam's money was paid back`

`Why passive?`

- We know what happened.
- We do not know who did it.
- The hidden person makes the story a mystery.

`Past deduction`

- `must have been found`
- high-confidence guess about the past
- passive because Sam does not know who found it

`Percentage joke`

- `50 per cent a thief`
- `25 per cent a thief`
- `100 per cent honest`
- Honesty is not really a percentage.

`Clear conscience`

- guilty -> partial repayment -> full repayment -> claims honesty

Avoid turning passive voice into a dry form chart. It should feel like the
grammar of an anonymous mystery.
