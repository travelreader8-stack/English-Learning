# Lesson 15 Plan - Good News

This is the source-of-truth design card for producing Lesson 15. A development
thread should read `AGENTS.md`, then `LESSON_PRODUCTION_PLAN.md`, then this file
before producing any Lesson 15 assets.

## Basic Info

- Lesson: 15
- Title: `Good news`
- Chinese title: `佳音`
- Core bridge: `这不是单纯的办公室故事，而是一次心理反转：老板严肃地叫我谈话，我以为坏事轮到我了，结果最后竟然是加薪。`

## Production Warnings

This lesson works only if the student feels the narrator's nervousness before
the final good news.

- Do not reveal the raise too early.
  - The script should first build the "bad news" expectation.
  - The final `extra thousand pounds a year` should land as a reversal.
- Do not make the business context too adult or heavy.
  - Explain layoffs and salaries simply.
  - Avoid long corporate explanations.
  - The child only needs to know: if business is bad and many people have left,
    an employee may worry about losing the job.
- Make the modern bridge student-friendly.
  - A child may not understand "boss calls employee into office".
  - Bridge to: teacher asks you to come after class, parent says "let's talk",
    coach calls you over, or class teacher looks serious.
- Do not turn the lesson into career anxiety.
  - Keep the tone tense but light.
  - The ending is warm and funny.
- Student-facing display:
  - Normalize `Mr.Harmsworth` to `Mr. Harmsworth` where possible in scripts and
    displays.
  - Explain `firm` as `company`.
  - Explain `salary` as regular pay for work.

If production requires editing `web/data/lessons.json`, stop and ask for a
shared-data edit because it is outside the normal per-lesson write set.

## Lesson Positioning

This lesson is about expecting bad news and receiving good news.

The secretary tells the narrator that Mr. Harmsworth wants to see him. The
narrator feels nervous when he enters the office. Mr. Harmsworth does not look
up from his desk. After the narrator sits down, the boss says that business is
bad and the firm cannot afford such large salaries. Twenty people have already
left. The narrator thinks his turn has come. He tries to speak weakly, but Mr.
Harmsworth stops him: "Don't interrupt." Then the boss smiles and says the
narrator will receive an extra thousand pounds a year.

The courseware should make the student understand:

`serious meeting -> bad business -> large salaries -> twenty people left -> my turn -> don't interrupt -> smile -> extra money`

The ending should land as: "Good news feels extra good because every previous
clue sounded like bad news."

## One-Sentence Hook

The boss called him into the office, talked about bad business and people
leaving, and just when he thought he was next, the boss smiled and gave him a
raise.

## Story Focus

The tension chain is:

1. The secretary says Mr. Harmsworth will see the narrator.
2. The narrator enters the office and feels very nervous.
3. Mr. Harmsworth does not look up from his desk.
4. The narrator sits down.
5. Mr. Harmsworth says business is very bad.
6. He says the firm cannot afford large salaries.
7. Twenty people have already left.
8. The narrator thinks his turn has come.
9. He speaks in a weak voice.
10. Mr. Harmsworth says, "Don't interrupt."
11. Then Mr. Harmsworth smiles.
12. He says the narrator will receive an extra thousand pounds a year.

The production should make the student feel the misdirection:

`Everything sounds like bad news until the final sentence changes the meaning.`

## Office And Money Bridge

This section should appear early in the script.

Explain:

- `secretary` here means an office worker who helps arrange meetings and pass
  messages.
- `Mr. Harmsworth would see me` means the boss wants to meet the narrator.
- `firm` means a company.
- `salary` means regular pay for work.
- `business was very bad` means the company is not doing well.
- If a company cannot afford salaries and people have already left, an employee
  may fear being fired or losing the job.

Suggested student-friendly bridge:

`你可以把这个办公室场景换成学校场景：老师突然说，下课后来我办公室一下。你进去以后，老师表情很严肃，还一直说最近情况不太好。你心里会不会想：完了，是不是轮到我挨批了？这篇课文就是这种心理，只不过发生在公司办公室。`

## Modern Bridge

Connect the old office scene to present-day experiences:

- a teacher asks a student to come after class
- a parent says seriously, "we need to talk"
- a coach calls someone aside after a game
- a club leader or class monitor announces that some people are being removed
  from a team
- a student thinks they are in trouble but actually gets praise, a chance, or a
  reward

The durable pattern is:

`authority calls you in -> serious tone -> you expect blame -> final reveal is praise or reward`

Do not center the lesson on adult employment. The child's emotional access point
is being called in by someone with authority.

## Main Language Focus

The main grammar is reported speech / indirect speech.

Teach it as retelling what other people said:

- `The secretary told me that Mr. Harmsworth would see me.`
- `He said that business was very bad.`
- `He told me that the firm could not afford to pay such large salaries.`
- `Then he smiled and told me I would receive an extra thousand pounds a year.`

Student-facing explanation:

`这篇课文像是在复述一次办公室谈话。作者不是直接把老板的话全部写成引号，而是用 told me / said that 把别人说过的话转述出来。`

Keep the grammar practical:

- `told me that...` = 告诉我某件事
- `said that...` = 说某件事
- `would` often appears because the whole story is being told in the past

Secondary grammar:

- Past perfect as earlier background:
  - `After I had sat down...`
  - `Twenty people had already left.`
  - `my turn had come.`

Do not let the secondary grammar overpower the story tension.

## Vocabulary Plan

Formal vocab blocks should be exactly 3:

1. `nervous`
   - Meaning: worried, tense, or afraid about what may happen.
   - Bridge: before a test, before a teacher meeting, before hearing a result.
   - Example: `I felt very nervous when I went into his office.`
2. `afford`
   - Meaning: have enough money to pay for something.
   - Bridge: a company may or may not afford salaries; a family may or may not
     afford an expensive thing.
   - Example: `The firm could not afford to pay such large salaries.`
3. `interrupt`
   - Meaning: stop someone while they are speaking.
   - Bridge: the narrator wants to speak, but the boss stops him.
   - Example: `Don't interrupt.`

Supporting vocabulary to introduce in context, not as full vocab cards:

- `secretary` = 秘书 / 办公室工作人员
- `firm` = company, 公司
- `salary` = regular pay for work
- `weak voice` = a voice without confidence
- `extra` = additional, more than before
- `business` = company activity / business situation
- `my turn had come` = now it was my time; here, "轮到我倒霉了"

## Scene And Script Direction

The script should feel like a small office suspense story with a warm reversal.

Recommended sequence:

1. Hook: "He thought he was about to receive bad news."
2. Modern bridge: teacher/parent/coach calls you in seriously.
3. Office context: secretary, boss, firm, salary.
4. Retell in 4 frames:
   - Frame 1: secretary tells the nervous narrator that Mr. Harmsworth will see
     him.
   - Frame 2: narrator enters the office; boss does not look up.
   - Frame 3: boss talks about bad business, large salaries, and twenty people
     leaving.
   - Frame 4: boss smiles and gives the unexpected raise.
5. Discussion: why every clue points to bad news before the final reversal.
6. Passage reading.
7. Vocab: `nervous`, `afford`, `interrupt`.
8. Grammar: reported speech as retelling an office conversation.
9. Living-scene transfer.
10. Outro: practice order, including read-aloud steps.

## Visual Direction

Keep visuals tense but friendly.

- Use a quiet office, desk, secretary, boss, and nervous employee.
- The boss can look serious at first but should not look cruel.
- Show the narrator's imagination/fear lightly, not as real disaster.
- The final frame should feel visibly warmer: smile, relief, good news.
- Avoid dark thriller lighting or corporate cruelty.
- Avoid making the office too luxurious or too modern; a simple workplace is
  enough.

Four storyboard frames:

1. Secretary gives the message: Mr. Harmsworth wants to see the narrator.
2. Nervous narrator enters the office; Mr. Harmsworth looks down at his desk.
3. The boss explains bad business and large salaries; the narrator worries that
   his turn has come.
4. The boss smiles and announces an extra thousand pounds a year.

## Living-Scene Exercise

The student-facing transfer should be about expecting bad news but receiving
good news.

Good prompts:

- `有没有一次老师或家长很严肃地叫你过去，你以为要挨批，结果是好消息？`
- `如果班主任说“下课后来一下”，你会怎么想？最后如果是表扬，会是什么感觉？`
- `写一个小场景：你以为是 bad news，最后发现是 good news。`

Expected scaffold:

`I felt very nervous when ___. I thought ___. Then ___ smiled and told me ___.`

Keep it emotionally safe. The exercise should not ask the student to describe a
painful punishment. It should focus on a small misunderstanding or surprising
good result.

## Read-Aloud Guidance

Choose 5-8 sentences that carry the tension and reversal.

Recommended sentences:

1. `The secretary told me that Mr. Harmsworth would see me.`
   - Focus: `told me that`, `would see me`
2. `I felt very nervous when I went into his office.`
   - Focus: `very nervous`
3. `He did not look up from his desk when I entered.`
   - Focus: quiet tension
4. `He told me that the firm could not afford to pay such large salaries.`
   - Focus: `could not afford`, `large salaries`
5. `Twenty people had already left.`
   - Focus: `had already left`
6. `I knew that my turn had come.`
   - Focus: narrator's fear
7. `Don't interrupt, he said.`
   - Focus: short, firm line
8. `Then he smiled and told me I would receive an extra thousand pounds a year!`
   - Focus: final good news and relief

The read-aloud section should help the student hear the emotional arc:

`message -> nervous -> serious boss -> bad business -> my turn -> don't interrupt -> smile -> raise`

## Grammar Handling

Do not start from mechanical reported-speech transformations. Start from the
reason the text uses reported speech: the narrator is retelling a meeting.

Suggested explanation:

`这篇课文不是现场直播，而是事后讲述。所以作者用 The secretary told me that... / He said that... / He told me that... 来转述别人说过的话。`

Use a compact display:

| Who spoke | Reported sentence | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| secretary | told me that Mr. Harmsworth would see me | 秘书通知我要见老板 |
| boss | said that business was very bad | 老板说生意不好 |
| boss | told me that the firm could not afford... | 老板说公司付不起高工资 |
| boss | told me I would receive... | 老板说我要加薪 |

Then connect to `would`:

`因为整件事发生在过去，原来要发生的将来动作，在转述里常常变成 would。`

Keep this explanation short and useful. The story's suspense matters more than
the full grammar chart.

## Tone

Light suspense, then relief.

The narration can make the student's inner voice vivid:

- "Uh-oh, why does he want to see me?"
- "Business is bad... salaries are too high... twenty people left... is it my
  turn?"
- "Wait, he smiled?"

But do not make the scene frightening. The final feeling should be relief and a
small laugh.

The child should feel:

- "I understand why the narrator was nervous."
- "I can see how the writer builds bad-news expectation."
- "The title Good news makes sense only after the ending."
- "Reported speech helps retell what people said in the meeting."

## Acceptance Criteria For Production

Lesson 15 production is successful only if:

- The script clearly frames the text as bad-news expectation turning into good
  news.
- The modern bridge connects office anxiety to a student-friendly authority
  meeting.
- The office bridge explains `secretary`, `firm`, `salary`, and `afford` simply.
- The 4 retell frames show secretary message, nervous office entry, bad-business
  talk, and final raise.
- The 3 formal vocab blocks are `nervous`, `afford`, and `interrupt`.
- Supporting words include `secretary`, `firm`, `salary`, `weak voice`, `extra`,
  `business`, and `my turn had come`.
- The grammar section teaches reported speech through retelling a meeting.
- Past perfect appears only as supporting action order.
- The living-scene exercise asks the student to describe expecting bad news but
  receiving good news.
- The final raise lands as the title's `Good news`.
- The outro includes the full practice order and the concrete read-aloud steps:
  `⓪ 跟读：听课文全文 -> 听原音 -> 录一句 -> 获取反馈`, then
  `① 生活场景`, `② 完形`, `③ 中译英`, `④ 英译中`, `⑤ 默写`.
