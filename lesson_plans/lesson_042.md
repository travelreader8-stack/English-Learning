## Production Warnings

This lesson should feel colorful and culturally curious, not exoticizing.

- Explain the geography before the snake scene.
  - `Delhi` is a large Indian city / capital territory.
  - `Old Delhi` refers to the older, historic part of Delhi, especially the
    traditional city area associated with Shahjahanabad.
  - `New Delhi` is the later planned administrative capital area.
  - For the student, the useful contrast is: Old Delhi = old city, markets,
    dense street life; New Delhi = planned capital / government area.
- Do not present India as strange or backward.
  - The scene should be framed as a traveler's glimpse of a different city
    rhythm and public street culture.
  - Avoid "mysterious India" language.
  - Avoid implying that snake charming represents all Indian culture.
- Add a light animal-welfare / modern-view note.
  - This is an old textbook scene about a traditional street performance.
  - Today we should also be aware of animal protection and should not encourage
    imitating or romanticizing animal performance.
- Add a scientific correction.
  - The story jokes that the snake cannot tell music styles apart.
  - In reality, snakes are not music listeners in the human sense.
  - The snake is more likely responding to the pipe's movement, vibration, and
    the performer's motion.
- Keep visuals safe and non-frightening.
  - The snake should look calm and stylized, not scary.
  - No dangerous close-ups, bites, venom, or threatening action.
- Explain `pipe` in context.
  - It is the snake charmer's wind instrument / long pipe, not a water pipe.
- Explain title tone.
  - `Not very musical` is humorous understatement: the snake cannot tell Indian
    music from jazz or pop.
- Do not edit `web/data/lessons.json` during normal lesson production.

## Lesson Positioning

This lesson is about tourists in Old Delhi watching a traditional street
performance and misunderstanding what makes the snake move.

The travelers have had a long walk through one of the markets of Old Delhi, so
they stop at a square to rest. They notice a snake charmer with two large
baskets on the other side of the square and go to watch him. When the performer
sees them, he picks up a long pipe covered with coins and opens one basket. As
he plays a tune, they get their first glimpse of the snake. The snake rises out
of the basket and follows the movements of the pipe. Then the performer
surprises them by playing jazz and modern pop songs. The snake continues to
"dance" slowly. The joke is that it obviously cannot tell the difference between
Indian music and jazz.

The courseware should make the student understand:

`Old Delhi market -> long walk -> square rest -> snake charmer -> two baskets -> long pipe with coins -> first glimpse of snake -> follows pipe movements -> jazz and pop -> snake still dances -> cannot tell the difference`

The ending should land as:

`The people notice the music changes, but the snake reacts the same way because
it is not really appreciating music.`

## One-Sentence Hook

In a busy square in Old Delhi, a snake seems to dance to music, but the joke is
that it cannot tell Indian music from jazz.

## Old Delhi / New Delhi Geography Bridge

This bridge should appear before the story begins.

Explain:

- `Delhi` is the large city / capital region.
- `Old Delhi` means the older historic city area, not simply a dirty or bad
  place.
- Old Delhi is associated with older markets, narrow streets, dense public life,
  historic buildings, and traditional street scenes.
- `New Delhi` is the later planned administrative capital area, with broader
  roads, government buildings, and a more planned city layout.
- In this lesson, the travelers are in Old Delhi, so the market, square, crowd,
  and street performance make sense.

Student-friendly explanation:

`德里像一座有很多历史层的城市。Old Delhi 是老城、市场、传统街景和历史建筑更集中的地方；New Delhi 是后来规划出来的首都行政区。课文说 one of the markets of old Delhi，是让我们进入一个游客在老城区市场里走很久、然后到广场休息的画面。`

Suggested visual:

`India 印度 -> Delhi 德里 -> Old Delhi 旧德里 -> market 市场 -> square 广场 -> snake charmer 耍蛇人`

Keep the geography short and visual. The goal is to give the student a map in
the mind, not to turn the lesson into a history lecture.

## Indian Street Culture And City Atmosphere Bridge

This lesson has a different atmosphere from many earlier lessons set in Britain
or Europe.

Explain gently:

- Earlier lessons often happen in homes, shops, hospitals, stations, offices, or
  other orderly indoor / institutional spaces.
- This lesson is more public and street-based: market, square, performer,
  baskets, pipe, coins, music, tourists, and an animal.
- In this scene, city life feels open, noisy, colorful, and sensory.
- Traditional performance and everyday public life appear together in the same
  street space.
- This is not a high/low comparison with English-speaking countries. It is a
  different cultural setting and city rhythm.

Suggested teacher-student dialogue:

Teacher:
`前面很多课，我们看到的是英国的家、商店、餐桌、医院、车站。这一课突然到了印度旧德里。你觉得画面有什么变化？`

Student:
`更热闹，有市场，有广场，还有表演。`

Teacher:
`对。这里的生活好像更多发生在街上。市场、广场、游客、表演者都在公共空间里。这不是说哪个文化更好，而是城市气质不同。旧德里给人的感觉更有声音、颜色和街头生活。`

Teacher:
`不过我们也要有现代眼光：课文记录的是一种传统街头表演。今天看到动物表演时，我们也会想到动物保护的问题。`

Then return to the joke:

`The funny point is not "India is strange." The funny point is that the people
think the music style matters, but the snake reacts the same way.`

## Snake Charming Science Bridge

This should be explained briefly and clearly.

Student-friendly explanation:

`课文说蛇跟着 pipe 的 movement 动。这个词很关键。蛇不是像人一样在欣赏旋律，也不是听懂了 Indian music、jazz 或 pop songs。它更可能是在跟着管子的移动、表演者的动作和周围震动做反应。所以标题 Not very musical 是幽默：它不是音乐家，当然分不出印度音乐和爵士乐。`

Key teaching contrast:

- Human listeners notice `Indian music`, `jazz`, and `modern pop songs`.
- The snake continues to move slowly because the visual / movement signal stays
  important.
- The word `dance` is in quotation marks because it is not really dancing like
  a human dancer.

Do not make the scientific correction too long. It should sharpen the joke, not
replace the story.

## Modern Bridge

Connect the old travel performance scene to present-day experiences:

- visiting a place with a very different street culture
- seeing a performance and first thinking it is magical
- learning to ask what is really happening
- understanding that tourists may misunderstand what they see
- respecting a culture while also applying modern science and animal-welfare
  thinking
- noticing that old textbooks record scenes from a different time

The durable pattern is:

`strange new scene -> first surprise -> closer observation -> scientific / humorous correction`.

Do not make the lesson anti-tradition. The balance is:

`curiosity + respect + clear thinking`.

## Main Language Focus

The main grammar / structure is time sequencing with `As`, `As soon as`, and
`When`, plus the tricky past perfect phrase `had had`.

### `had had a long walk`

Explain lightly:

`As we had had a long walk...`

- First `had` = past perfect helper.
- Second `had` = main verb meaning `experienced / taken`.
- Meaning: `After we had already walked for a long time...`

Student-facing explanation:

`两个 had 放在一起并不是写错了。第一个 had 是语法帮手，告诉你“在过去某个时间之前已经发生”；第二个 had 才是“经历了一次长途步行”的 had。`

Do not over-teach past perfect. Teach enough so the student is not frightened by
the repeated word.

### Time Connectors

Use the story as a sequence board:

| Connector | Story sentence | Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `As` | `As we had had a long walk...` | gives the reason / background |
| `After a time` | `After a time, we noticed...` | moves time forward |
| `As soon as` | `As soon as he saw us...` | immediate action |
| `When` | `When he began to play a tune...` | moment when the snake appears |
| `however` | `The snake, however, continued...` | contrast / surprise |

### `tell the difference between A and B`

Make this a practical expression:

- `tell the difference between Indian music and jazz`
- `tell the difference between British English and American English`
- `tell the difference between real and fake`
- `tell the difference between similar words`

Student-facing explanation:

`tell the difference between A and B` means `分辨 A 和 B 的区别`. Here the snake
cannot tell the difference because it is not listening like a human.

Secondary language points:

- `have a rest`
  - stop and rest.
- `at the other side of the square`
  - across the square.
- `have a look at`
  - go and see.
- `covered with coins`
  - coins are attached / decorating it.
- `first glimpse`
  - first quick look.
- `rose out of the basket`
  - came up from the basket.
- `continued to dance`
  - kept dancing.
- `obviously`
  - clearly.

## Vocabulary Plan

Formal vocab blocks should be exactly 3:

1. `snake charmer`
   - Meaning: a traditional street performer who appears to make a snake move
     by playing a pipe.
   - Bridge: explain this as a cultural performance scene, while also adding a
     modern animal-welfare and science note.
   - Example: `We noticed a snake charmer with two large baskets.`
2. `glimpse`
   - Meaning: a quick, brief look.
   - Bridge: the travelers do not see the whole snake clearly at first; they get
     their first quick look as it rises from the basket.
   - Example: `We had our first glimpse of the snake.`
3. `difference`
   - Meaning: how two things are not the same.
   - Bridge: teach the phrase `tell the difference between A and B`.
   - Example: `It could not tell the difference between Indian music and jazz.`

Supporting vocabulary to introduce in context, not as full vocab cards:

- `musical` = good at / sensitive to music; here used humorously
- `old Delhi` = older historic part of Delhi
- `market` = busy place where people buy and sell goods
- `square` = open public place in a city
- `have a rest` = rest for a while
- `basket` = container, here for the snake
- `pipe` = long wind instrument in this story
- `covered with coins` = decorated with coins
- `tune` = simple piece of music
- `rose out of` = came up from
- `movement` = action of moving
- `jazz` = a style of music
- `modern pop songs` = popular modern songs
- `continue` = keep doing something
