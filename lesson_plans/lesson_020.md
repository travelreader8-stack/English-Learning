# Lesson 20 Plan - One Man in a Boat

This is the source-of-truth design card for producing Lesson 20. A development
thread should read `AGENTS.md`, then `LESSON_PRODUCTION_PLAN.md`, then this file
before producing any Lesson 20 assets.

## Basic Info

- Lesson: 20
- Title: `One man in a boat`
- Chinese title: `独坐孤舟`
- Core bridge: `这不是一课关于“钓鱼技术很差”的课，而是一课关于别人看结果、自己享受过程：朋友只看到他没有钓到鱼，他真正享受的是坐在船上什么也不做的安静时间。`

## Production Warnings

This lesson should be about process versus result, not about fishing skills.

- Do not make the narrator look stupid or incompetent.
  - He is not upset because he was never really going for fish.
  - The joke is that his friends misunderstand the point of the activity.
- Explain `sport` carefully.
  - `Fishing is my favourite sport` here means hobby/outdoor activity, not a
    competitive school sport or Olympic event.
- Keep `old boots and rubbish` as comic exaggeration.
  - It should feel funny and slightly absurd, not dirty or disgusting.
- Explain `empty bag` as the fishing bag after a morning on the river.
  - It is empty because he caught no fish.
- Do not frame `doing nothing at all` as laziness.
  - In this story, it is quiet enjoyment and rest.
- Avoid making the moral too heavy.
  - The lesson should let the child recognize that some activities are valuable
    because of the experience, not only the output.

If production requires editing `web/data/lessons.json`, stop and ask for a
shared-data edit because it is outside the normal per-lesson write set.

## Lesson Positioning

This lesson is about a man whose friends judge his hobby by the wrong standard.

The narrator says fishing is his favourite sport. He often fishes for hours and
catches nothing. This does not worry him. Other unlucky fishermen may catch old
boots and rubbish instead of fish. He is even less lucky: he never catches
anything, not even old boots. After whole mornings on the river, he always goes
home with an empty bag. His friends tell him to give up fishing because it is a
waste of time. But they do not realize the important point: he is not really
interested in fishing. He is interested in sitting in a boat and doing nothing
at all.

The courseware should make the student understand:

`fishing hobby -> no fish -> friends judge by results -> narrator does not worry -> real interest is quiet time`

The ending should land as: "He is not failing at fishing; he is succeeding at
having the quiet morning he actually wants."

## One-Sentence Hook

He never catches fish, but he keeps going because the fish were never the real
point.

## Story Focus

The process-versus-result chain is:

1. The narrator says fishing is his favourite sport.
2. He fishes for hours without catching anything.
3. This does not worry him.
4. Some unlucky fishermen catch old boots and rubbish instead of fish.
5. He is even less lucky and never catches anything.
6. After whole mornings on the river, his bag is empty.
7. His friends tell him to give up fishing.
8. They call it a waste of time.
9. They do not realize one important thing.
10. He is not really interested in fishing.
11. He is only interested in sitting in a boat and doing nothing at all.

The production should make the contrast visible:

`friends' scoreboard: no fish` versus `narrator's real goal: quiet boat time`.

## Culture And Scene Bridge

This section should appear early in the script.

Explain:

- Recreational fishing can be a quiet outdoor hobby, not only a way to get food.
- In older countryside or riverside scenes, a person might sit alone in a small
  boat for a whole morning.
- Friends may judge the activity by the catch, but the narrator judges it by the
  feeling of peace.
- `sport` can mean a hobby or leisure activity, not just competitive sport.

Suggested student-friendly bridge:

`这篇课文里的钓鱼，对很多孩子可能有点远。可以把它换成现在的事情：有人玩 Minecraft，不一定是为了通关；有人画画，不一定是为了得奖；有人搭积木，不一定是为了搭出最完美的作品。他可能只是喜欢慢慢做、安静待着。Lesson 20 的钓鱼也是这样：朋友看“有没有鱼”，他自己要的是“坐在船上，什么也不做”。`

## Modern Bridge

Connect the fishing scene to present-day experiences:

- building in Minecraft without trying to win
- drawing quietly without entering a competition
- walking, cycling, or sitting outside just to clear the mind
- assembling LEGO slowly for the process, not the final display
- practicing piano or basketball because the routine feels good, not only for a
  prize
- taking photos or making small videos without needing likes
- sitting alone and doing nothing after a busy school day

The durable pattern is:

`other people judge the result -> I value the process -> English explains the real interest`

Do not turn the lesson into anti-achievement preaching. Keep it warm and
balanced: results can matter, but some activities are also valuable as rest,
attention, and quiet time.

## Main Language Focus

The main grammar is `-ing` forms used as activity nouns, especially after
prepositions.

Teach it through repeated story phrases:

- `without catching anything`
  - `without` is followed by `catching`.
  - Meaning: he fishes for hours, but catches nothing.
- `Instead of catching fish`
  - `instead of` is followed by `catching`.
  - Meaning: not fish, but old boots and rubbish.
- `After having spent whole mornings on the river`
  - `after` can be followed by an `-ing` phrase.
  - Meaning: after he has spent the whole morning on the river.
- `interested in fishing`
  - `interested in` is followed by `fishing`.
  - Meaning: interested in the activity.
- `interested in sitting in a boat and doing nothing`
  - The real interest is the quiet activity, not the result.

Student-facing explanation:

English often uses `-ing` when an action is treated like a thing or an activity.
In this lesson, `fishing`, `catching`, `sitting`, and `doing` are not single
actions in one second; they are activities the narrator is talking about.

Use a compact pattern display:

| Pattern | Example | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| without + -ing | without catching anything | not catching |
| instead of + -ing | instead of catching fish | not A, but B |
| after + -ing | after having spent whole mornings | after that activity |
| interested in + -ing | interested in sitting in a boat | interested in the activity |

Secondary language point:

- `not even`
  - Meaning: the result is even less than the lowest expectation.
  - In this lesson: not even old boots.
- `at all`
  - Adds emphasis to a negative or "nothing" idea.
  - In this lesson: `doing nothing at all`.

## Vocabulary Plan

Formal vocab blocks should be exactly 3:

1. `catch`
   - Meaning in this lesson: get a fish with a line; more generally, get or
     grab something.
   - Bridge: catch a ball, catch a fish, catch a bus.
   - Example: `without catching anything`
2. `waste`
   - Meaning: use time, money, or effort badly.
   - Bridge: `a waste of time` when someone thinks no useful result came out.
   - Example: `It's a waste of time.`
3. `realize`
   - Meaning: understand or become aware of something.
   - Bridge: finally understand the real reason.
   - Example: `They don't realize one important thing.`

Supporting vocabulary to introduce in context, not as full vocab cards:

- `fishing` = catching fish as a hobby/activity
- `sport` = hobby/outdoor activity in this lesson
- `fisherman` = a person who catches fish
- `boot` = a kind of shoe that covers the foot and lower leg
- `rubbish` = trash
- `unlucky` = not lucky
- `instead of` = rather than
- `empty bag` = bag with nothing in it
- `give up` = stop doing something
- `interested in` = care about / enjoy
- `doing nothing at all` = doing absolutely nothing

## Scene And Script Direction

The script should feel like a gentle reveal of the narrator's real goal.

Recommended sequence:

1. Hook: "He spends whole mornings fishing and catches nothing, but he is not
   worried."
2. Bridge from fishing to modern process-focused hobbies.
3. Explain why friends think it is a waste of time.
4. Retell in 4 frames:
   - Frame 1: narrator sits alone in a small boat on a quiet river with fishing
     gear.
   - Frame 2: comic contrast: other unlucky fishermen catch old boots or
     rubbish instead of fish.
   - Frame 3: narrator goes home with an empty bag while friends tell him to
     give up fishing.
   - Frame 4: narrator peacefully sits in the boat doing nothing, revealing
     that this is what he wanted.
5. Discussion: why the friends and narrator judge the same activity
   differently.
6. Passage reading.
7. Vocab: `catch`, `waste`, `realize`.
8. Grammar: `-ing` as activity after `without`, `instead of`, `after`, and
   `interested in`.
9. Living-scene transfer.
10. Outro: practice order, including read-aloud steps.

## Visual Direction

Keep visuals calm, comic, and river-focused.

- Use one consistent narrator, one small boat, and one quiet river across the
  frames.
- Suggested storyboard `visual_consistency`:
  `Keep the same narrator and boat across all four frames. The narrator is a
  relaxed adult man in his thirties, slim build, short dark hair, wearing a
  soft olive jacket, rolled-up sleeves, and a straw or canvas fishing hat. He
  has a calm, slightly amused face rather than a frustrated face. The boat is
  the same small wooden rowboat with simple fishing gear and a brown empty
  fishing bag. Keep the river setting gentle, green, quiet, and early-morning;
  no readable text anywhere.`
- The tone should be restful, not slapstick-heavy.
- The old boots and rubbish can appear as a mild comic image, but avoid dirty or
  unpleasant details.
- The final frame should show peaceful idleness as the point of the story.
- Avoid crowded fishing competition imagery.
- Avoid making the narrator look foolish.

Four storyboard frames:

1. The narrator fishes alone from a small wooden boat on a quiet river.
2. A gentle comic scene shows unlucky fishermen catching old boots or harmless
   rubbish instead of fish.
3. The narrator carries an empty bag home while friends gesture that he should
   stop.
4. The narrator sits peacefully in the boat with the fishing rod idle, enjoying
   doing nothing at all.

## Living-Scene Exercise

The student-facing transfer should be about enjoying an activity for the
process, not only the result.

Good prompts:

- `你有没有做一件事，不是为了赢，也不是为了完成得最快，而是因为过程本身很舒服？`
- `别人觉得这是 a waste of time，但你自己真正感兴趣的是什么？`
- `用英语说清楚：I am not really interested in ___. I am interested in ___.`

Expected scaffold:

`I am not really interested in ___. I am only interested in ___ and ___.`

Add result-versus-process contrast:

`My friends think it is a waste of time, but they don't realize that I enjoy ___.`

Good scene choices:

- building slowly in Minecraft
- drawing quietly
- walking alone
- building LEGO
- practicing a hobby without competing
- sitting quietly after school

Keep the exercise personal and calm. The student should learn to explain the
real reason behind an activity.

## Read-Aloud Guidance

Choose 5-8 sentences that carry the no-result comedy and the final reveal.

Recommended sentences:

1. `Fishing is my favourite sport.`
   - Focus: `fishing`, `favourite sport`
2. `I often fish for hours without catching anything.`
   - Focus: `without catching anything`
3. `But this does not worry me.`
   - Focus: calm contrast
4. `Instead of catching fish, they catch old boots and rubbish.`
   - Focus: `Instead of catching`
5. `I never catch anything -- not even old boots.`
   - Focus: `not even`
6. `After having spent whole mornings on the river, I always go home with an empty bag.`
   - Focus: `After having spent`, `empty bag`
7. `It's a waste of time.`
   - Focus: friends' judgment
8. `They don't realize one important thing.`
   - Focus: reveal setup
9. `I am only interested in sitting in a boat and doing nothing at all!`
   - Focus: final reveal, `interested in`, `doing nothing at all`

If a shorter read-aloud set is needed, prioritize sentences 2, 4, 5, 7, 8, and
9.

The read-aloud section should help the student hear the contrast:

`without catching anything -> empty bag -> waste of time -> don't realize -> doing nothing at all`

## Grammar Handling

Do not start from a full gerund chart. Start from the repeated `-ing` rhythm in
the story.

Suggested explanation:

`这篇课文里，很多动作都变成了一种“活动”：fishing, catching, having spent, sitting, doing。英语里在 without, instead of, after, interested in 后面，经常接 -ing。因为这里说的不是一个孤立动作，而是一个活动、一段过程、一种兴趣。`

Use a compact display:

| Phrase | Why `-ing`? |
| --- | --- |
| without catching anything | after `without` |
| Instead of catching fish | after `instead of` |
| After having spent whole mornings | after `after` |
| interested in fishing | after `interested in` |
| interested in sitting and doing nothing | activities as interests |

Then connect to meaning:

`The grammar points to the theme: this man is not counting fish. He is talking
about activities and quiet time.`

## Tone

Quiet, lightly funny, and reflective.

The child should feel:

- "I understand why not catching fish does not worry him."
- "I can see the joke in old boots and rubbish."
- "I know `a waste of time` is the friends' opinion, not the whole truth."
- "I can use `interested in + -ing` to explain what I really enjoy."
- "The ending is peaceful, not lazy or stupid."

Avoid:

- mocking the narrator for catching nothing
- making fishing technique the main point
- overexplaining environmental rubbish
- turning `doing nothing` into a moral failure
- making the final line sound like an excuse

- The script clearly frames the lesson as process versus result, not a fishing
  tutorial.

  Minecraft, drawing, LEGO, walking, quiet time, or non-competitive practice.
- `sport` is explained as hobby/outdoor activity in this lesson.

- The friends' `waste of time` judgment is contrasted with the narrator's real
  interest.
- The 4 retell frames show quiet fishing, comic unlucky catches, empty-bag
  friend criticism, and peaceful doing-nothing reveal.
- The storyboard includes `visual_consistency` for the narrator, the small
  wooden boat, fishing gear, empty bag, and river setting.
- The 3 formal vocab blocks are `catch`, `waste`, and `realize`.
- Supporting words include `fishing`, `sport`, `fisherman`, `boot`, `rubbish`,
  `instead of`, `empty bag`, `give up`, `interested in`, and
  `doing nothing at all`.
- The grammar section teaches `-ing` forms through story phrases:
  `without catching`, `instead of catching`, `After having spent`,
