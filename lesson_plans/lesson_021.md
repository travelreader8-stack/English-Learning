# Lesson 21 Plan - Mad or Not?

This is the source-of-truth design card for producing Lesson 21. A development
thread should read `AGENTS.md`, then `LESSON_PRODUCTION_PLAN.md`, then this file
before producing any Lesson 21 assets.

## Basic Info

- Lesson: 21
- Title: `Mad or not?`
- Chinese title: `是不是疯了`
- Core bridge: `这不是单纯的“机场很吵”，而是一课关于现代设施改变普通人的生活：机场启用以后，飞机日夜飞过，邻居搬走，补偿金来了，主人公却坚持留下。`

## Production Warnings

This lesson should be about noise, public infrastructure, and personal
stubbornness, not about fear of planes.

- Explain `mad` as a colloquial exaggeration.
  - `driving me mad` means making me extremely annoyed or almost unable to bear
    it.
  - Do not treat `mad` here as a serious medical diagnosis.
- Explain British spelling.
  - `aeroplane` is British English; `airplane` and `plane` are more familiar to
    many modern students.
- Do not turn the lesson into a political debate about airports or relocation.
  - The story's teaching focus is the narrator's situation and language, not a
    policy judgement.
- Keep the plane imagery safe.
  - The narrator imagines the house being knocked down by a passing plane, but
    the story should not become a crash scene.
- Explain `large sum of money` as compensation / money offered to move away.
  - Do not imply the narrator has already accepted it.
- Explain `must have been driven away` carefully.
  - It means the narrator strongly guesses that over a hundred people have been
    forced to leave by the noise.
  - It does not mean "they must be driven away now".
- Keep the ending slightly comic and weary.
  - Everybody says he must be mad; he admits they are probably right.

If production requires editing `web/data/lessons.json`, stop and ask for a
shared-data edit because it is outside the normal per-lesson write set.

## Lesson Positioning

This lesson is about a person whose home is being overwhelmed by a new airport.

The narrator lives near an airport. Passing planes can be heard night and day.
The airport was built years ago, but at first it could not be used. Last year it
came into use. Since then, the noise has driven many people away from their
homes. The narrator is one of the few people left. Sometimes he even imagines
that his house will be knocked down by a passing plane. He has been offered a
large sum of money to go away, but he is determined to stay. Everyone says he
must be mad, and he says they are probably right.

The courseware should make the student understand:

`airport built -> airport comes into use -> noise day and night -> neighbours leave -> money offered -> narrator stays`

The ending should land as: "He knows staying may look unreasonable, but he is
determined to stay in his home."

## One-Sentence Hook

The airport finally opened, the neighbours left, the noise never stopped, and
one man still refused to move.

## Story Focus

The pressure chain is:

1. Aeroplanes are slowly driving the narrator mad.
2. He lives near an airport.
3. Passing planes can be heard night and day.
4. The airport was built years ago.
5. For some reason, it could not be used then.
6. Last year, it came into use.
7. Over a hundred people must have been driven away by the noise.
8. The narrator is one of the few people left.
9. Sometimes he thinks the house will be knocked down by a passing plane.
10. He has been offered a large sum of money to go away.
11. He is determined to stay.
12. Everybody says he must be mad, and he half-agrees.

The production should make the contrast visible:

`external pressure grows` versus `the narrator refuses to leave`.

## Culture And Scene Bridge

This section should appear early in the script.

Explain:

- An airport can change life for people living nearby: more planes, more noise,
  more traffic, and sometimes relocation.
- A place can be built before it is actually used. `came into use` means it
  started operating.
- Noise can push people away from a home even when the building itself is still
  standing.
- Being offered money to move away is like a compensation offer; it does not
  mean the person wants to leave.

Suggested student-friendly bridge:

`这课里的机场，不要只想成旅行出发的地方。对住在旁边的人来说，机场也可能是日夜不断的噪音。现在孩子更容易理解的场景是：小区旁边修了高架桥、地铁工地、铁路、商场或机场。设施对城市有用，但住得很近的人生活会被改变。Lesson 21 讲的就是这种冲突：别人都搬走了，主人公拿到一大笔钱也不走。`

## Modern Bridge

Connect the airport scene to present-day experiences:

- a new elevated road built near an apartment
- subway construction outside a school or home
- a railway or airport becoming busier after opening
- a shopping mall or stadium bringing traffic and noise
- construction noise during exam season
- neighbours moving away because the environment changed
- someone refusing to leave a familiar home even when it becomes inconvenient

The durable pattern is:

`new facility -> constant noise -> people leave -> compensation offered -> determined choice`

Do not over-politicize the story. Keep it focused on how English describes
external forces, passive events, and a person's decision to stay.

## Main Language Focus

The main grammar is passive voice used to show that people and things are being
affected by outside forces.

Teach it through the narrator's lack of control:

- `passing planes can be heard night and day`
  - The focus is the noise being heard, not who hears it.
- `The airport was built years ago`
  - The focus is the airport, not the builders.
- `it could not be used then`
  - The focus is the airport not being used.
- `Over a hundred people must have been driven away`
  - The narrator strongly guesses that people were forced away by the noise.
- `this house will be knocked down`
  - The focus is the imagined result to the house.
- `I have been offered a large sum of money`
  - The focus is the offer received by the narrator.

Student-facing explanation:

Passive voice is useful when the sentence focuses on what happens to someone or
something. In this lesson, the narrator is not controlling the airport, the
planes, the noise, or the offer. Things keep happening to his home and to the
people around him.

Use a compact display:

| Passive sentence | What is affected? |
| --- | --- |
| planes can be heard | the noise experience |
| the airport was built | the airport |
| it could not be used | the airport |
| people must have been driven away | the neighbours |
| the house will be knocked down | the house |
| I have been offered money | the narrator |

Secondary language points:

- `must have been driven away`
  - Meaning: the narrator is almost sure this already happened.
  - This is strong guess about the past plus passive voice.
- `came into use`
  - Meaning: started being used / started operating.
- `determined to stay`
  - Meaning: has made a firm decision to stay.

## Vocabulary Plan

Formal vocab blocks should be exactly 3:

1. `mad`
   - Meaning in this lesson: extremely annoyed or almost unable to stand
     something.
   - Bridge: `The noise is driving me mad.`
   - Example: `Aeroplanes are slowly driving me mad.`
2. `sum`
   - Meaning: an amount of money.
   - Bridge: `a large sum of money` means a lot of money, often as one offer or
     payment.
   - Example: `I have been offered a large sum of money.`
3. `determined`
   - Meaning: having firmly decided to do something.
   - Bridge: determined to stay, determined to finish, determined to try again.
   - Example: `I am determined to stay here.`

Supporting vocabulary to introduce in context, not as full vocab cards:

- `aeroplane` = British English for airplane / plane
- `airport` = place where planes take off and land
- `passing planes` = planes flying past
- `night and day` = all the time
- `reason` = why something happens
- `come into use` = start being used
- `drive away` = force someone to leave
- `noise` = unwanted sound
- `knock down` = destroy or make fall down
- `offer` = give or propose
- `go away` = leave
- `probably` = likely

## Scene And Script Direction

The script should feel like a personal story about modern noise pressure.

Recommended sequence:

1. Hook: "The planes never stop, the neighbours are gone, and one man still
   stays."
2. Bridge from airport noise to modern construction/road/railway noise.
3. Explain `aeroplane`, `came into use`, and the compensation offer.
4. Retell in 4 frames:
   - Frame 1: narrator's house near an airport with planes heard night and day.
   - Frame 2: airport that was built years ago but only came into use last year.
   - Frame 3: neighbours leaving because of the noise while the narrator remains.
   - Frame 4: narrator receives a large money offer but stands determined at
     his house.
5. Discussion: why everybody thinks he is mad, and why he still stays.
6. Passage reading.
7. Vocab: `mad`, `sum`, `determined`.
8. Grammar: passive voice as outside forces affecting people and things.
9. Living-scene transfer.
10. Outro: practice order, including read-aloud steps.

## Visual Direction

Keep visuals noisy but not frightening.

- Use one consistent narrator, one consistent house, and one airport/flight path
  setting across the frames.
- Suggested storyboard `visual_consistency`:
  `Keep the same narrator and house across all four frames. The narrator is a
  tired but stubborn middle-aged man, average build, short greying hair, wearing
  a brown cardigan over a pale shirt. His expression moves from exhausted to
  determined, but his face and clothing stay consistent. The house is the same
  modest old home with a small garden near an airport flight path. Keep passing
  planes visible but safe, high enough in the sky; show noise through motion,
  posture, and atmosphere, not through readable words.`
- The planes should not crash or appear to hit the house.
- The house should feel lived-in and emotionally important.
- The money offer can be shown as an envelope or formal document with no
  readable text.
- Neighbours leaving should feel sad and practical, not chaotic.
- Avoid disaster imagery or political protest scenes.

Four storyboard frames:

1. Narrator stands tired outside his house as planes pass overhead.
2. The airport nearby is shown active after years of disuse, with planes taking
   off in the distance.
3. Neighbours move away with bags or boxes while the narrator stays by his home.
4. Narrator holds a money-offer envelope but stands firmly in front of his
   house, determined to stay.

## Living-Scene Exercise

The student-facing transfer should be about being affected by outside noise or
pressure, then deciding whether to stay, leave, stop, or continue.

Good prompts:

- `你有没有被外面的噪音、施工、作业或某件反复发生的事弄得很烦？`
- `别人觉得你应该放弃或离开，但你有没有决定继续坚持？`
- `用英语说清楚：什么东西 is driving me mad，别人 offered you 什么选择，你为什么 are determined to stay / continue。`

Expected scaffold:

`The ___ is driving me mad. I have been offered ___. But I am determined to ___.`

Add a lighter student-life version:

`This homework is driving me mad, but I am determined to finish it.`

Keep the exercise emotionally safe. It can be about noise, homework, practice,
or a hard project; it does not need to be about moving house.

## Read-Aloud Guidance

Choose 5-8 sentences that carry the noise pressure, passive voice, and final
decision.

Recommended sentences:

1. `Aeroplanes are slowly driving me mad.`
   - Focus: `driving me mad`
2. `I live near an airport and passing planes can be heard night and day.`
   - Focus: `can be heard`, `night and day`
3. `The airport was built years ago, but for some reason it could not be used then.`
   - Focus: `was built`, `could not be used`
4. `Last year, however, it came into use.`
   - Focus: `came into use`
5. `Over a hundred people must have been driven away from their homes by the noise.`
   - Focus: `must have been driven away`
6. `I am one of the few people left.`
   - Focus: isolation
7. `I have been offered a large sum of money to go away.`
   - Focus: `have been offered`, `large sum`
8. `I am determined to stay here.`
   - Focus: `determined to stay`
9. `Everybody says I must be mad and they are probably right.`
   - Focus: weary comic ending

If a shorter read-aloud set is needed, prioritize sentences 1, 2, 5, 7, 8, and
9.

The read-aloud section should help the student hear the pressure chain:

`driving me mad -> can be heard -> was built -> came into use -> driven away -> offered money -> determined to stay`

## Grammar Handling

Do not start from a passive-voice formula table. Start from what the narrator
cannot control.

Suggested explanation:

`这篇课文里，主人公不是主动做很多事的人。他是被外部环境影响的人。飞机 can be heard，机场 was built、could not be used，邻居 must have been driven away，房子可能 will be knocked down，他自己 has been offered 一大笔钱。被动语态在这里不是为了复杂，而是因为故事讲的是：现代设施和噪音正在改变一个人的生活。`

Use a compact display:

| Active force | Passive effect |
| --- | --- |
| passing planes | can be heard night and day |
| builders / authorities | airport was built |
| some reason | it could not be used |
| noise | people were driven away |
| offer makers | I have been offered money |

Then add the special pattern:

`must have been driven away` = I strongly guess they have already been forced to
leave.

## Tone

Noisy, weary, stubborn, and slightly comic at the end.

The child should feel:

- "I understand why the airport noise is unbearable."
- "I know `mad` here is an everyday exaggeration."
- "I can see why people moved away."
- "I understand that the money offer is meant to make him leave."
- "I can use passive voice to say someone or something is affected by outside
  forces."

Avoid:

- making the lesson anti-airport or anti-development
- making the planes look dangerous or crashing
- treating `mad` as a serious diagnosis
- turning the money offer into a detailed legal discussion
- teaching passive voice as a long mechanical formula list

## Acceptance Criteria For Production

Lesson 21 production is successful only if:

- The script clearly frames the lesson as modern facility noise changing a
  person's life, not just "airplanes are loud".
- The modern bridge connects airport noise to roads, railways, construction,
  subways, malls, stadiums, or other nearby facilities.
- `aeroplane` is explained as British English for airplane / plane.
- `driving me mad` is explained as everyday exaggeration, not a medical label.
- `came into use` is explained as started operating / started being used.
- `large sum of money` is explained as a money offer or compensation to move
  away.
- The 4 retell frames show house near airport, airport coming into use,
  neighbours leaving, and narrator refusing the money offer.
- The storyboard includes `visual_consistency` for the narrator, his house, and
  the airport/flight path setting.
- The 3 formal vocab blocks are `mad`, `sum`, and `determined`.
- Supporting words include `aeroplane`, `airport`, `passing planes`,
  `night and day`, `reason`, `came into use`, `driven away`, `noise`,
  `knocked down`, `offered`, and `probably`.
- The grammar section teaches passive voice through outside forces affecting
  people/things: `can be heard`, `was built`, `could not be used`,
