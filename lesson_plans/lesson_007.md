## Basic Info

- Lesson: 7
- Title: `Too late`
- Chinese title: `为时太晚`
- Core bridge: `机场钻石包裹很电影化，但“贵重物运输链、快递追踪、开箱验货、发现早已被调包”很现代。`

## Production Warnings

This lesson should be vocabulary-rich. The student has limited vocabulary, and
the story only becomes clear if the airport / parcel / crime words are grouped
and explained.

- Do not make `Too late` mean only "the plane was late". The real title means
  the detectives were too late: the diamonds had already been replaced.
- Do not present this as action-movie violence. Keep it as a clean detective
  puzzle about timing, security, and a parcel that has been switched.
- Explain `Customs House` as the customs inspection area where goods are checked
  when they enter a country.
- Explain `airfield` as the plane-side area outside the main passenger building.
- Explain `South Africa` briefly as a country far away, so the parcel has gone
  through a long transport chain.
- The ending should clearly reveal that the problem happened before the parcel
  was opened at the airport. Guarding the last door was not enough.

If production requires editing `web/data/lessons.json`, stop and ask for a
shared-data edit because it is outside the normal per-lesson write set.

## Lesson Positioning

This lesson is not mainly about airports or diamonds. It is a detective story
about a security chain.

The detectives know a valuable parcel of diamonds is coming from South Africa.
Someone has warned the police that thieves will try to steal the diamonds. So
the detectives wait at the airport all morning. Some wait inside the main
building; others wait on the airfield. They guard the door while the parcel is
opened.

But when the parcel is opened, it is already full of stones and sand. The
detectives were watching the final step, but the key event had already happened
earlier.

For a child today, connect this to deliveries and unboxing:

- a valuable phone, tablet, game console, watch, or camera being shipped
- tracking a package through many steps
- watching the delivery person arrive, but still needing to check what is inside
- opening a box and discovering it has been switched or filled with the wrong
  thing
- learning that security has to cover the whole chain, not only the last moment

## One-Sentence Hook

Imagine everyone is waiting at the airport for a valuable parcel of diamonds.
The detectives guard the door carefully. Then they open the parcel and find only
stones and sand.

## Story Punchline

The detective chain is:

1. The plane is late.
2. Detectives wait at the airport all morning.
3. They are expecting a valuable parcel of diamonds from South Africa.
4. A few hours earlier, someone warned the police that thieves would try to
   steal the diamonds.
5. When the plane arrives, detectives cover two places: the main building and
   the airfield.
6. Two men carry the parcel into the Customs House.
7. Two detectives keep guard at the door.
8. Two other detectives open the parcel.
9. The precious parcel is full of stones and sand.

The ending should land as: "They were ready for a theft at the airport, but the
diamonds were already gone."

## Modern Bridge

Do not ask only "Have you seen diamonds?" or "Have you been to an airport?" The
modern bridge is package security and checking the whole process.

Useful modern scenes:

- You order an expensive device online and track the delivery.
- The package arrives, but you still need to open it and check the contents.
- A delivery box looks normal from the outside, but the inside may be wrong.
- Security cameras and guards at the final door cannot fix a problem that
  happened earlier in the chain.
- A warning arrives before the event, but the action may still be too late.

The transferable idea is:

`important object -> long delivery chain -> warning -> guard the final step -> open the box -> too late`

## Vocabulary Strategy

This lesson should deliberately teach more vocabulary than Lessons 3-6.

Keep exactly 3 formal `vocab` blocks so the course structure remains stable, but
use retell, discussion, read-aloud focus notes, and the living-scene exercise to
explain many supporting words.

### Formal Vocab Blocks

Use exactly 3 vocab blocks. Prefer:

1. `detective`
   - A person who investigates clues and solves crimes; not just any police
     officer.
2. `parcel`
   - A package. Bridge directly to modern delivery and unboxing.
3. `keep guard`
   - To watch and protect a place or object so no one can steal or enter.

### Required Supporting Vocabulary

The script should also explain these words in context:

- Airport / transport chain:
  - `airport` - 机场
  - `airfield` - 停机坪、飞机停靠区域
  - `main building` - 主楼
  - `Customs House` - 海关检查货物的地方
  - `South Africa` - 南非，说明包裹来自很远的地方
- Valuable object / contents:
  - `valuable` - 有价值的、贵重的
  - `precious` - 珍贵的，强调重要和价值
  - `diamonds` - 钻石
  - `stones and sand` - 石头和沙子，是结尾反转
- Crime / action:
  - `thief / thieves` - 小偷、盗贼
  - `steal` - 偷走
  - `expecting` - 正在等待、期待某物到来
  - `opened the parcel` - 打开包裹检查

### Vocabulary Grouping

Do not dump the words as a list. Group them as a story map:

1. **Airport and transport**
   `airport / airfield / main building / Customs House / South Africa`
2. **Valuable parcel**
   `valuable / precious / parcel / diamonds / stones and sand`
3. **Detective action**
   `detectives / expecting / thieves / steal / keeping guard / opened`

The student should come away with a usable word network, not isolated flashcard
memory.

## Language Focus

Use these language points:

- `detectives were waiting at the airport all morning`
  - They were not just there for one moment; they were waiting for a long period.
- `were expecting a valuable parcel of diamonds`
  - They were waiting for something specific and important.
- `someone had told the police`
  - The warning happened before the airport scene.
- `thieves would try to steal the diamonds`
  - From the earlier warning, this was the expected future danger.
- `while others were waiting...`
  - At the same time, different detectives were in different places.
- `keeping guard`
  - Active protection, not simply standing there.
- `To their surprise`
  - Sets up the final reveal.

## Grammar Point

Recommended grammar point: detective-story time layers.

Do not teach this as a tense chart. Teach it as a film timeline:

- Earlier:
  - `someone had told the police...`
  - The warning came before the plane arrived.
- During the waiting scene:
  - `detectives were waiting...`
  - `some were waiting... while others were waiting...`
  - Several actions were happening over the same period.
- Expected danger:
  - `thieves would try to steal...`
  - From the police's point of view, the theft was still going to happen.
- Final reveal:
  - `the parcel was full of stones and sand`
  - The real theft or switch had already happened before the final check.

Student-friendly explanation:

`A detective story often needs three clocks: what had already happened, what people were doing at that time, and what they expected would happen next.`

## Read Aloud Sentences

Use 7 guided sentences. The goal is to reinforce the vocabulary network while
making the detective timeline easy to say aloud.

- `The plane was late and detectives were waiting at the airport all morning.`
  - focus: `detectives`, `airport`, and the long waiting feeling.
- `They were expecting a valuable parcel of diamonds from South Africa.`
  - focus: `expecting`, `valuable parcel`, `diamonds`, `South Africa`.
- `A few hours earlier, someone had told the police that thieves would try to steal the diamonds.`
  - focus: `A few hours earlier`, `had told`, `thieves`, `steal`.
- `When the plane arrived, some of the detectives were waiting inside the main building while others were waiting on the airfield.`
  - focus: split at `while`; contrast `main building` and `airfield`.
- `Two men took the parcel off the plane and carried it into the Customs House.`
  - focus: `took the parcel off the plane`, `Customs House`.
- `While two detectives were keeping guard at the door, two others opened the parcel.`
  - focus: `keeping guard` and `opened the parcel`.
- `To their surprise, the precious parcel was full of stones and sand!`
  - focus: `To their surprise`, `precious parcel`, `stones and sand` as the reveal.

## Living-Scene Exercise

Use `mode: "all_fills"` for Lesson 7, consistent with Lessons 1-6.

The exercise should transfer the story into a modern delivery / unboxing /
security-chain scenario. The goal is to use the new vocabulary, not to create a
violent crime scene.

Suggested word bank:

- `detective`
- `valuable parcel`
- `diamonds`
- `thieves`
- `steal`
- `keeping guard`
- `opened the parcel`
- `stones and sand`
- `too late`
- `Customs House`

Suggested three fill scenes:

- Delivery check:
  `The box looked normal, but when we ___, it was full of old paper.`
  Expected: `opened the parcel`
- Security chain:
  `The guard was ___ at the final door, but the package had already been changed earlier.`
  Expected: `keeping guard`
- Detective reveal:
  `The ___ expected a ___, but the box was full of ___. It was ___.`
  Expected: `detective`, `valuable parcel`, `stones and sand`, `too late`

Adjust wording if needed so the expected phrases appear exactly in the word
bank and fit naturally. Use singular/plural carefully: if the sentence says
`The ___ expected`, the answer should be singular `detective`; if the sentence
uses `The ___ were waiting`, the answer should be plural `detectives`.

## Visual Direction

Use 4 retell frames:

1. A delayed plane and detectives waiting at an airport, with the feeling of a
   long morning. Include both airport interior and runway/airfield hints if
   possible, but no readable signs.
2. A secure valuable parcel arriving from far away. Show a sealed package being
   handled carefully, with subtle diamond/security feeling but no readable text.
3. Detectives split across the main building and airfield / Customs House,
   guarding the final step and watching the parcel.
4. The opened parcel reveals stones and sand instead of diamonds; detectives
   look surprised, emphasizing the clean puzzle reveal rather than danger.

No text, letters, captions, readable signs, or visible English words in images.
Avoid guns, violence, dramatic explosions, or frightening criminals. The visual
style should be detective-puzzle suspense, not action thriller.

## Tone

Suspenseful, clear, and puzzle-like. Let the child feel the structure of a
detective story: warning, waiting, guarding, opening, and surprise.

Because this is a vocabulary-rich lesson, the script should slow down and give
quick Chinese meanings for key scene words as they appear. It should still feel
like a story, not a dictionary page.
